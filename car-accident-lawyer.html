    Negligence, Fault, & Liability after a Car Accident in Los Angeles

    Most car accident cases are based on negligence. This means that another driver or some other party acted unreasonably or failed to act and caused you else harm.

    Car Accident Evidence & Documentation

    It may seem obvious who’s at fault in your car accident. However, you must prove that someone was speeding, distracted, or intoxicated to have a successful claim.

    The burden of proof for a civil lawsuit is based on a preponderance of the evidence, which means that enough evidence exists to prove that the other party is more likely than not to have caused your injuries. Photos, eyewitness testimonies, and other forms of evidence could be used to prove fault.

    Liability & Fault in a Los Angeles Car Accident

    Your injuries alone aren’t enough to prove liability. To determine liability, you must establish the four elements of negligence. Someone must owe you a duty of care, fail to uphold that duty, and cause your damages, which require monetary compensation.

    Los Angeles is an at-fault state for liability in a car accident. In most cases, you will file a third-party claim with the responsible driver’s insurance company or their employer if they were working at the time.
